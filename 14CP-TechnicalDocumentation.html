What is technical Documentation & Why is it important to review examples of technical documents?
Technical documents describe the framework, functionality, or purpose of a product, service, or system. From persuasive content to educative articles, there are a wide variety of technical documents you may write. Reviewing examples of technical documents can help you decide on a suitable type to create for your specific situation. In this article, we explore why it is important to review technical document examples, discover tips for creating technical documents, and present 15 technical document examples. Reviewing examples of technical documents is important because they can help learn how to communicate complex information to others. They're common in software, medicine, and manufacturing. These documents may describe a product's development process or relate to products in their finished state. Regardless of the field, it's typically the responsibility of a technical writer to create these documents.
Create an Outline
An outline is a plan highlighting sections to cover in a technical document. 
            Creating one can help you organize your thoughts before you start writing.
Be Concise
Being brief in the content you create can aid your reader's understanding of technical documents.
             You can make your writing more direct by rephrasing sentences and correcting words that may affect its clarity.
Proofread for Errors
 Correct grammar, spelling, and punctuation errors before submitting technical documents. 
            Consider using specialized software or asking a colleague to edit your work.
Understand the target audience
Research your reader to determine what words to use in technical documents. For example, 
            if you're writing code documentation for computer programmers in your team, you may use technical terms they understand.
Conduct extensive research
 It's essential the information you include is factual. 
            You can collaborate with other technical professionals to check the technical document's accuracy.
Maintain consistency
Aim to use a unified tone, layout, format, and structure throughout the technical document.
             For example, it may be best to use the same font in all sections of a technical document, emphasizing only headers.
